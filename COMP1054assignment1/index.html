Open Sans

About

Commissioned by Google and designed by Steve Matteson in 2010,Open Sans is a humanist sans serif typeface that was released in 2011. It's based upon his earlier design of Droid Sans that he developed for use on Android. Open sans consistently ranks among the top Google Fonts with over 4 billion views per day on more than 20 million websites totaling more than 1.5 trillion views per year.

It's an open-source, free typeface optimized for print, web, and mobile interfaces. It is easily legible at almost all sizes, simple and user friendly with a very favorable appearance. This can be attributed to the cosideration of vertical stress, Open forms and having a neutral friendly appearance. It features tall character letters, with a wide aperature for open counter letters and features true italics.

Open sans has 879 different characters covering ISO Latin 1, Latin CE, Greek and Cyrillic alphabets with many diacritics. Open Sans has 6 different Weights: Light, Normal, Medium, Semi-bold, Bold, and Extra Bold. Each weight also has a reciprocating italic version making a total of 12 versions. There are also 4 deriviatives of Open Sans, though they arent all free.

Open sans is popular among web developers following a flat design style. Although Open sans is so popular you should take care to use it only when appropiate. Because of its simple design it can great for content or things you want to get across to the user, But if you use it exclusively youll be missing out on other fonts that may be appropiate.
